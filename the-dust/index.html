Jill stood at the oven—her shoulders hung low as the tattered jacket hanging on the nail near the door. Todd lazed into the kitchen and sat down at the table. Bacon lingered in the air flirting with the manure. Jill turned around and looked at her husband, stared at him. She put her hand down on the counter and let out a noiseless sigh.

“What will you do today, Todd?”

Todd slouched in his chair; his right arm plopped on the table fingering a glass of scotch. He looked at his wife’s cracked, brown feet—she wished he could look at her face, her eyes, green as the leaves on the cornstalk—and turned his ear up to the cicada that just started singing.

“Todd, what will you do?”
